# AI Tells — the catalogue

A running list of patterns that make writing read as AI-generated, with
before/after examples. This is the heart of the skill and will be expanded as we
work. Treat these as guidance for building judgment, not a rigid find-and-replace
list — context decides whether something is actually a problem.

> **Status:** seed list. We'll refine, add examples, and add counter-examples
> (cases where a "tell" is actually fine) as we iterate.

## 1. Filler transitions and throat-clearing

Empty connective tissue that adds words without meaning.

- "It's important to note that…", "It's worth mentioning…"
- "In today's fast-paced world…", "In the ever-evolving landscape of…"
- "When it comes to…", "At the end of the day…"

## 2. The "it's not just X, it's Y" construction

The signature LLM rhetorical move. Also: "It's not about X. It's about Y."

- *Before:* "This isn't just a tool — it's a complete solution."
- *After:* "This tool handles the whole job."

## 3. Inflated / corporate vocabulary

Words that sound impressive but say little.

- "leverage" → use
- "utilize" → use
- "delve into" → look at / dig into
- "robust", "seamless", "cutting-edge", "game-changing", "elevate", "unlock"

## 4. Hedging and over-qualification

- "It's generally considered that…", "Some might argue…", "arguably"
- Stacking qualifiers: "This can sometimes potentially help in certain cases."

## 5. Em-dash overuse and rule-of-three padding

- Em-dashes used as a default connector in every other sentence.
- Lists of three adjectives where one would do: "fast, reliable, and efficient."

## 6. Empty conclusions / summary bows

- "In conclusion…", "Ultimately…", "By following these steps, you'll be well on
  your way to…"
- Paragraphs that restate what was just said without adding anything.

## 7. Over-symmetry and parallelism

Suspiciously balanced sentence structures, repeated sentence openings, every
paragraph the same length and shape.

## 8. Vague enthusiasm

- "This is a great way to…", "powerful", "exciting", emoji-as-punctuation.
- Generic praise that could apply to anything.
